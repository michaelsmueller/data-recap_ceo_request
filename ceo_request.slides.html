Olist Report¶

Date: April 2025

Project Goal: Increase the profit by identifying and addressing underperforming sellers and improving customer satisfaction through data-driven insights.

Business Context & Objective¶

Olist operates a large marketplace, aggregating a wide variety of sellers and products into a unified customer platform. While Olist earns:

  • A 10% commission on every product sold (excluding freight)
  • And a monthly subscription fee of 80 BRL per seller

its costs are deeply affected by customer satisfaction, particularly via poor review scores, which can reduce repeat purchase rates, increase return/refund handling, or harm brand reputation.

Additionally, maintaining a large number of sellers and order volume drives IT infrastructure costs, which scale with:

  • (number of sellers)
  • (number of items sold)

Thus, our core mission was to determine:

"How can Olist maximize profit by improving review scores and managing seller performance?"

Data Preparation¶

We constructed a dataset by combining features across the following dimensions:

Seller Information:

  • seller_id, seller_city, seller_state

Delivery Performance:

  • delay_to_carrier, wait_time

Activity & Sales Metrics:

  • date_first_sale, date_last_sale, months_on_olist
  • n_orders, quantity, quantity_per_order
  • sales

Customer Feedback:

  • share_of_five_stars, share_of_one_stars
  • review_score

Exploratory Data Analysis¶

We analyzed the distribution of all numerical variables, focusing on identifying:

Key Focus Areas:

  • Outliers
  • Skewed distributions
  • Correlation with review_score

Findings:

  • wait_time is positively skewed and shows a strong inverse correlation with review scores
  • Some sellers have consistently poor performance (low sales, long delays, high share of 1-star reviews)
  • Review score median is 5, but there's a visible tail of sellers pulling averages down

Regression Modeling (OLS)¶

We used Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) regression to model how numerical features affect the review_score.

Features Used:

  • wait_time
  • delay_to_carrier
  • quantity_per_order
  • price
  • freight_value
  • seller_state (as dummies for a second model)

Results:

  • wait_time is the most significant negative predictor (p < 0.001)
  • delay_to_carrier also had a negative but weaker effect
  • seller_state was not statistically significant after controlling for logistics variables
  • Model R was low (~0.19), indicating customer reviews are also influenced by unobservable factors (e.g., product quality, communication)

Variance Inflation Factor¶

To ensure our regression models were not distorted by multicollinearity, we ran a VIF analysis.

All variables had acceptable VIF < 5, meaning we can trust the regression coefficients.

Variable VIF
wait_time 1.76

Profitability & IT Cost Model¶

We computed total revenues, costs, and profits per seller using:

  • Revenues = 0.10 sales + 80 months_on_olist
  • Cost of reviews = bad review rate orders BRL cost (1 star = 100 BRL, 2 stars = 50 BRL, 3 stars = 40 BRL)
  • Profit = Revenues Cost of reviews

IT costs increase with marketplace size and complexity:

  • IT_cost = (n_sellers) + (n_items)
  • = 3,157.27 (seller cost factor)
  • = 978.23 (item cost factor)

Key insights:

  • Many sellers are net unprofitable, costing more in bad reviews than they generate in revenue
  • Nonlinear IT growth implies that keeping low-contributing sellers increases costs disproportionately
  • Square root scaling shows diminishing marginal cost for additional sellers/items

What-if Scenario: Remove Sellers¶

We simulated what would happen if Olist ranked and removed its least profitable sellers:

Steps:

  • Sort sellers by descending profit (pre-IT cost)
  • Compute cumulative profit and IT costs
  • Calculate net profit = cumulative profit IT cost
  • Plot net profit vs number of sellers

Key Business Takeaways¶

Optimal Seller Strategy:

  • Keep top 2,139 sellers (ranked by profitability)
  • Remove 17.7% of sellers from gross profit maximum
  • Max net profit: 1,093,619 BRL (+1.0% improvement)

Cost Structure at Optimal Point:

  • Gross profit: 1,491,819 BRL
  • IT costs: 398,200 BRL (26.7% of gross profit)
  • Items sold: 66,456 (only 1.9% reduction)

Strategic Recommendations:

  • Focus on delivery performance to improve review scores
  • Implement stronger SLAs for seller shipping times
  • Develop continuous seller performance monitoring
  • Prioritize marketplace quality over quantity

Recommendations¶

Remove underperforming sellers

  • Sellers beyond the 2,139 cutoff reduce overall profit
  • Removing them raises margins and lowers IT costs

Strengthen delivery SLAs (service level agreements)

  • Require prompt delay_to_carrier handling
  • Monitor seller shipping behavior

Implement continuous seller scoring

  • Combine revenue, review scores, and delivery metrics
  • Create automatic alerts or offboarding triggers

Optimize IT cost scaling

  • Olist should grow seller base strategically, not blindly
  • Invest in sellers with proven ROI metrics

Conclusion¶

This analysis demonstrated that:

  • Not all sellers are good for business
  • Delivery performance is central to customer satisfaction
  • Olist can grow profits by managing seller quality rather than quantity

Limitations:

  • Some external factors (e.g., product category, customer behavior) not included
  • Text reviews were not analyzed
  • Static snapshot: no seasonal trends or seller growth stages

By cutting unnecessary costs and rewarding high-performing sellers, Olist can become a more efficient and customer-trusted marketplace.

Category Profitability Analysis¶

Objective:

  • Analyze which product categories contribute to Olist's profitability
  • Identify categories that should be eliminated
  • Maximize net profit by optimizing category mix

Methodology:

  • Calculate revenue per category (10% sales commission + subscription fees)
  • Determine cost of bad reviews per category
  • Apply IT cost model to category combinations
  • Find optimal subset of categories that maximize net profit

Results: Categories to Remove¶

12 categories should be removed, representing:

  • 242 sellers (8.0% of seller base)
  • 19,489 items (17.7% of catalog)
  • Total loss: -45,001 BRL

Top 5 unprofitable categories:

  1. Bed, Bath & Table: -20,557 BRL (127 sellers)
  2. Telephony: -14,556 BRL (58 sellers)
  3. Electronics: -4,890 BRL (38 sellers)
  4. Audio: -2,361 BRL (3 sellers)
  5. Home Comfort: -1,740 BRL (3 sellers)

Common factors: High competition, low margins, higher review costs

Optimization Results¶

Keeping only 55 out of 67 categories would:

  • Increase net profit by 12.5%
  • Retain 92.0% of sellers
  • Retain 82.3% of products
  • Reduce IT costs through more focused operations

Key insight: Unprofitable categories often have higher wait times (12.8 days vs. marketplace average) and slightly lower review scores (3.90 vs. marketplace average)

Recommendations for Category Management¶

Short-term actions:

  • Phase out the 12 identified unprofitable categories
  • Redirect affected sellers to more profitable categories
  • Improve delivery performance in remaining categories

Long-term strategy:

  • Establish regular category performance reviews
  • Set profitability thresholds for introducing new categories
  • Create category-specific SLAs based on profitability potential

Implementation approach:

  • Start with removing smallest unprofitable categories (fewer sellers affected)
  • Graduate to larger categories with appropriate seller transition plans

Comparing Optimizations¶

Seller optimization:

  • Removing the bottom 28% of sellers (keeping 2,139 of 2,967)
  • Increases net profit to 1.09M BRL

Category optimization:

  • Removing 18% of categories (keeping 55 of 67)
  • Increases net profit to 742K BRL

Conclusion: Targeted seller removal is more effective

  • Categories often contain mix of profitable and unprofitable sellers
  • Removing entire categories risks losing profitable opportunities

Implementation strategy:

  • First priority: Remove worst-performing individual sellers
  • Second priority: Reduce investment in consistently unprofitable categories
  • Long-term: Develop seller scoring system for each category's unique dynamics
